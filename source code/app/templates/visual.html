The Attacks

Different Types of Attacks

An attack is defined as international if the nationality of the perpetrator group is different from the location of the attack. Around 90% of attacks are international. An attack is considered unsuccessful depending on different tactics. For example, if a bomb is planted and fails to detonate or an assasin attempts and fails to kill the intended target, then an attack is not successful. About 90% of attacks are successful and more than 95% are suicide attacks.

The Victims

The Most Common Victim Types

Private citizens and properties are the most common victims of terrorist attacks. Within private citizens and properties, unnamed civilians make the highest percentage of both fatalities and injuries. This pattern may vary across countries. For example, business and goverment have had attacked the most and the police has the highest number of deaths and injuries in the US.
